Privacy Policy DFS Virtual Call Center (“DFS Virtual Call Center,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) values the privacy of the individuals who use our technology, websites, and related services (collectively, the “DFS Virtual Call Center Website”). This privacy policy will help you understand what information we collect and use at DFS Virtual Call Center, and the choices you have associated with that information. Information We Collect

A. What We Collect Device Information. DFS Virtual Call Center receives information from your devices, including IP address, location, web browser type, mobile operating system version, phone carrier and manufacturer, application installations, device identifiers, mobile advertising identifiers, push notification tokens, and, if you register with your social media account (such as Facebook, Google or LinkedIn), your applicable social media identifier.

Biometric Data. DFS Virtual Call Center may collect, store, use, and derive biometric data from your submitted pictures, driver's license, or government-issued ID. DFS Virtual Call Center will use this data to confirm your identity from time to time. . Service Data (including Session and Usage data).

To help us understand how you use the DFS Virtual Call Center Website and to help us improve it, we automatically receive information about your interactions with the DFS Virtual Call Center Website, like the pages or other content you view, your actions within the DFS Virtual Call Center Website, and the dates and times of your visits. When you use the DFS Virtual Call Center Website, we receive information generated through such use, either entered by you or others who use the Website with you (for example, schedules, attendee info, etc.), or from the DFS Virtual Call Center Website itself (for example, duration of session, use of webcams, connection information, servicing information, etc.). We may also collect usage and log data, including diagnostic data to help us support the services.

Call, Email and Text Information. We work with third-party partners to facilitate calls, emails and text messages to you. We may receive information about these communications including the date and time of the call, email or SMS message, and whether the call was answered or the message was received or opened. We will share your information with such partners to facilitate these communications and to help us provide services on the DFS Virtual Call Center Website.

Third Party Analytics. We use third party technologies, such as Facebook Analytics and Google Analytics, to help us to understand how you use the website and DFS Virtual Call Center Website and ways that we can improve your experience. These technologies may track things such as how long you spend on the site and the pages that you visit so we can continue to produce engaging content and improve the DFS Virtual Call Center Website and your experience. We also use social media buttons and plugins that allow you to connect with your social network in various ways, including Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn. These sites may set cookies through our site which may be used to enhance your profile on their site or contribute to the data they hold for various purposes outlined in their respective privacy policies.

Tracking Technologies. DFS Virtual Call Center and our partners use cookies and similar tracking technologies to track user traffic patterns and hold certain registration information. Tracking technologies also used are beacons, tags and scripts to collect and track information and to improve and analyze our service. Information from Cookies and Similar Technologies. DFS Virtual Call Center is continuously improving the DFS Virtual Call Center Website through the use of various third-party web analytics tools, which help us understand how visitors use our websites, desktop tools, and mobile applications, what they like and dislike, and where they may have problems. We collect information through the use of “cookies”, tracking pixels, and similar technologies to understand how you navigate through the DFS Virtual Call Center Website and interact with DFS Virtual Call Center advertisements, to learn what content is popular, and to save your preferences. Cookies are small text files that web servers place on your device; they are designed to store basic information and to help websites and apps recognize your browser. We may use both session cookies and persistent cookies. A session cookie disappears after you close your browser. A persistent cookie remains after you close your browser and may be accessed every time you use the DFS Virtual Call Center Website. You can consult your web browser(s) to modify your cookie settings. Please note that if you delete or choose not to accept certain cookies from us, you may be missing out on certain features of the DFS Virtual Call Center Website.

Examples of Cookies We Use: Cookie Type Purpose Description Session Cookies We use these cookies to operate our websites. Some cookies are essential for the operation of DFS Virtual Call Center websites. If a user chooses to disable these cookies, the user will not be able to access all of the content and features.

Preference Cookies We use these cookies to remember your preferences. When you register at a DFS Virtual Call Center website, we use cookies to store unique, randomly assigned user IDs that we generate for administrative purposes. These cookies enable you to enter the DFS Virtual Call Center sites without having to log on each time.

Advertising Cookies These cookies are used to serve you with advertisements that may be relevant to you and your interests. We use cookies to make advertising more engaging to users, as well as to assist in reporting on marketing program performance and analytics.

Security Cookies These cookies are used for general security purposes and user authentication. We use security cookies to authenticate users, prevent fraudulent use of login credentials, and protect user data from access by unauthorized parties.

How We Share the Information We Collect With Third Parties
Ensuring your privacy is important to us. We do not share your personal information with third parties except as described in this privacy policy. We may share your personal information with (a) third party service providers; (b) business partners; (c) affiliated companies within our corporate structure and (d) as needed for legal purposes. Third party service providers have access to personal information only as needed to perform their functions. Examples of how we may share information with service providers include:

• PROVIDING OUR SERVICES; • PAYMENT PROCESSING AND FRAUD PREVENTION; • PROVIDING CUSTOMER SUPPORT; • SENDING MARKETING COMMUNICATIONS; • CONDUCTING RESEARCH AND ANALYSIS; AND • PROVIDING CLOUD COMPUTING INFRASTRUCTURE.

Changes to Our Privacy Policy. We may make changes to this privacy policy from time to time. If we make any material changes, we will let you know through the DFS Virtual Call Center Website, by email, or other communication. We encourage you to read this privacy policy periodically to stay up-to-date about our privacy practices.
